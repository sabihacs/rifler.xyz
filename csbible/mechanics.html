Crosshair Placement

Proper crosshair placement will just net you frags, unironically, its that easy.

Proper crosshair placement is a god send, learning hwo to play positions and crosshair placements will just get you freelo, use the yprac maps for each map in your pool that you play and learn the common angles, drilling them for a week or two will lead you to a higher win rate taking fights on angles that you were unsure of, like proper banana pathing on inferno or proper angle clearing onto the a site on inferno, learning the proper pathing will make you more confident and will make the rounds more formulaic without randomness on your part.

Aspects of aim

  • clicking - clicking on static and dynamic targets
  • tracking - smooth tracking and evasive tracking are the primary ones you will play
  • switching - switching between targets

each of these have prominence in counter strike, static being peaking into players holding an angle, dynamic being players swinging you, tracking also applies to targets swinging you, switching is just multiple enemies on screen that you switch too its pretty self explanatory gang....

focus on each of these equally as you are only as strong as your weakest link

Consistency

At the end of the day its all routine, if you dont have a solid routine in place you will never really be consistant. if you want to adopt a routine one i've seen float around t1 and t2 players is

  • aimbotz warmup (just get warm and loose)
  • deathmatch / hs only deathmatch 250 kills to 500 kills focusing on methods such as spraying, tapping , flicks etc
  • kovaaks playlist or aimlabs playlist focusing on weakness or general tac shooter playlist

Committing to the routine for a long period of time will net results. after 30 days you are bound to be better than where you were when you started.

Mechanics will only carry you so far in this game to a point, once you get into higher skilled matches the skill is really in knowledge and team play, there is no point in being the sharpest person on the server if one flash is all it takes to take you out the round, you have to learn when and how to play anti and play off of others unironically.
