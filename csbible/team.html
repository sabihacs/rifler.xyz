General advice

just do your best to be mechanically flush and do your best to communicate well with the people you play with, its not that hard

basic respect and courtesy goes a really long way in making sure your playing time is enjoyable for everyone involved, be sure to respect the time you get from other teams and players when you practice with them

Think about the decisions you make at the end of the day, thats really all there is to it, always be thinking about what it is that you are doing and why you are doing them.

Communication

The other players dont see or hear what you do, you have to actively be comming the things you see and hear, in CS every piece of information is valuable and can shape decisions about a round, if you are holding B site on inferno and the CT and the Coffins smoke come down you need to comm that to get a rotate or even to just alert your team as to what is happening on your side of the map, there is no value in you not talking about what it is that you are seeing or hearing.

You should have set plays set out for your site partner that you can turn to and reference, the steam notes page is really useful for remembering setups that you have made.

Stages of the Round

There are 3 stages of a round

  • Early
  • Mid
  • Late

    • Early

    the ealiest stage of the round where the goal is to establish map control

    Mid

    the middle stage of the round where the goal is to hold map control and decide where to end

    Late

    the latest stage of the round where the goal is to execute onto a site

    Elastic band theory

    understanding that on CT and T there are zones of operation where majority of the plays should be made and when players need to play a more passive setup when they are not in the majority of the pack, this phase is prominent in the early and mid round stages

    If you are in the operation area look for fights / control of map if your are not in operation area play passive and make sure you stay alive.
